## 10. The OFA Foundation

The OFA Foundation is the governing body for the Licensed Work's community, contribution model, and commercial licensing operations.

### 10.1 Purpose

The OFA Foundation exists to:

a) Manage and distribute commercial license revenue among contributors.

b) Determine contribution shares through a transparent governance process.

c) Resolve disputes between contributors, between licensees and the Licensor, and any other disputes arising under this License.

d) Set policy for accepting, evaluating, and crediting contributions.

e) Maintain the integrity and direction of the Licensed Work.

### 10.2 Governance

The OFA Foundation operates under a governance structure defined in its Charter (a separate document). Until the Charter is ratified, the Licensor acts as sole governor.

The governance structure shall provide, at minimum:

a) A transparent process for calculating and disputing contribution shares.

b) Voting rights for contributors proportional to their contribution, with the Licensor retaining a permanent controlling interest.

c) Published records of revenue received and distributed.

d) A formal dispute resolution process, including mediation before any legal action.

### 10.3 Dispute Resolution

Any dispute arising under this License shall first be submitted to the OFA Foundation for mediation. If mediation fails to resolve the dispute within 90 days, either party may pursue legal remedies.

### 10.4 Membership
