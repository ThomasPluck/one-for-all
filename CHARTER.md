# OFA Foundation Charter v1.0

**Established by Thomas Pluck, 2026**

---

## 1. Mission

The OFA Foundation exists to:

a) Steward the development of the One-For-All (OFA) Parametric Layout Editor and related works.

b) Provide a robust anti-commercialization entity that maintains a copyleft front for electronic design automation (EDA) tooling in the open-source silicon ecosystem.

c) Fairly compensate contributors to the Licensed Work from commercial license revenue.

d) Ensure that open-source EDA tooling remains available and accessible to hobbyists, academics, and researchers without commercial encumbrance.

---

## 2. Membership

### 2.1 Membership Tiers

The Foundation recognises three tiers of membership:

**a) Founder**

Thomas Pluck holds the permanent and irrevocable status of Founder. There is exactly one Founder. The Founder role cannot be transferred, delegated, or dissolved except by the Founder's own written declaration.

**b) Contributors**

Any individual whose contribution to the Licensed Work has been accepted into the production codebase and who has signed the OFA Contributor Agreement. Contributor status is maintained by ongoing or historical contribution to the project.

**c) Licensees**

Any individual, organisation, or entity that has entered into a commercial license agreement with the Foundation and is in good standing (i.e., current on all payments and obligations).

### 2.2 Admission

Contributor membership is granted automatically upon the first accepted contribution to the production codebase and execution of the Contributor Agreement.

Licensee membership is granted upon execution of a commercial license agreement and receipt of initial payment.

### 2.3 Removal

Membership may be revoked by the Foundation for:

a) Violation of the OFA License.

b) Violation of this Charter.

c) Failure to meet financial obligations under a commercial license agreement (Licensees).

d) Conduct detrimental to the Foundation or the project, as determined by Foundation vote.

Removal of a Contributor or Licensee requires a Foundation vote as described in Section 3.

## 3. Governance and Voting

### 3.1 Voting Shares

Voting power in the Foundation is allocated as follows:

| Tier | Share | Allocation |
|------|-------|------------|
| Founder | 51% | Held solely by Thomas Pluck, permanently and irrevocably. |
| Contributors | 25% | Divided equally among all Contributors in good standing, allocated by internal vote among Contributors. |
| Licensees | 24% | Divided among Licensees in proportion to their cumulative financial contribution to the Foundation. |

### 3.2 Voting Procedures

a) **Ordinary resolutions** require a simple majority (>50%) of total voting shares.

b) **Charter amendments** require a supermajority of 75% of total voting shares.

c) The Founder may veto any resolution. A vetoed resolution is defeated regardless of vote count.

d) Votes may be cast in person, by video conference, or by written proxy submitted to the Foundation Secretary prior to the vote.

### 3.3 Quorum

A vote is valid only if the Founder participates and at least one other member from either the Contributor or Licensee tier is present or has submitted a proxy.

### 3.4 Meetings

The Foundation shall hold a general meeting at least once per calendar quarter. Additional meetings may be called by the Founder or by petition of members representing at least 20% of total voting shares.

Meeting agendas shall be published to all members at least 14 days in advance. Minutes shall be recorded and made available to all members within 14 days of the meeting.

## 4. Revenue Policy

### 4.1 Rate Schedule

The Foundation shall maintain and publish a commercial license rate schedule. The rate schedule specifies the default royalty rate or fee structure applicable to each category of Commercial Use as defined in the OFA License.

The initial rate schedule shall be set by the Founder. Subsequent changes to the rate schedule require an ordinary resolution.

### 4.2 Negotiated Agreements

The Foundation may negotiate individual commercial license agreements with terms differing from the published rate schedule. Such agreements require approval by the Founder or by an officer delegated this authority by the Founder.

### 4.3 Payment Terms

Commercial license fees are payable quarterly, within 30 days of the end of each calendar quarter. Licensees shall provide a revenue report detailing Applicable Revenue (as defined in the OFA License) for the quarter alongside payment.

The Foundation reserves the right to audit Licensee revenue reports at the Foundation's expense, no more than once per calendar year per Licensee, with reasonable notice.

### 4.4 Late Payment

Payments not received within 60 days of the due date shall incur interest at a rate of 2% per month on the outstanding balance. Payments not received within 120 days constitute a material breach of the commercial license agreement and may result in license termination and revocation of Licensee membership.

## 5. Revenue Distribution

### 5.1 Principle

Revenue distribution is determined by the Contributors collectively, including the Founder in their capacity as a Contributor. Each Contributor, regardless of the size of their contribution, holds one equal vote (1/N) in revenue distribution decisions.

### 5.2 Process

a) At each quarterly general meeting, the Foundation shall present the total commercial license revenue received for the preceding quarter, net of Foundation operating costs (as defined in Section 5.3).

b) Contributors shall review and discuss the relative value of contributions made during the period.

c) Revenue shares for the quarter shall be determined by a peer-review process among Contributors. Each Contributor must present and defend their claim to a share at the meeting.

d) The default presumption is an equal split among active Contributors, with adjustments for extraordinary contributions as agreed by the group.

e) Revenue distribution decisions require a simple majority vote among Contributors present at the meeting (1/N voting, not weighted by Section 3.1 shares).

f) The Founder participates in this process as a Contributor on equal footing with other Contributors. The Founder's 51% governance voting share under Section 3.1 does not apply to revenue distribution votes.

### 5.3 Foundation Operating Costs

Before revenue is distributed to Contributors, the Foundation shall deduct reasonable operating costs, including but not limited to:

a) Legal and accounting fees.

b) Infrastructure costs (hosting, CI/CD, tooling).

c) Administrative costs.

d) A reserve fund, capped at a percentage of annual revenue to be determined by ordinary resolution. Until otherwise resolved, the reserve fund cap is 10% of annual revenue.

Operating costs shall be reported transparently at each quarterly meeting.

### 5.4 Attendance Requirement

A Contributor must be present (in person, by video conference, or by written submission reviewed at the meeting) at the quarterly revenue distribution meeting to receive a share for that quarter. Contributors who do not attend and do not submit a written claim forfeit their share for that quarter, which is redistributed among attending Contributors.

This provision exists to ensure that revenue flows to active, engaged participants. It is not intended as a punitive measure, and the Foundation may grant exceptions for extenuating circumstances by simple majority vote of attending Contributors.

## 6. Contribution Assessment

### 6.1 Peer Review

The value of contributions is assessed through peer review by the Contributor body. There is no fixed formula. Contributors are expected to exercise professional judgement in evaluating the significance, complexity, and impact of each other's work.

### 6.2 Anti-Dilution

Refactoring, reimplementing, or duplicating the functionality of an existing contribution does not extinguish or reduce the original contributor's claim to a revenue share. Contributors are expected to recognise when new work builds upon or replaces existing work and to credit original contributors accordingly.

Disputes regarding dilution are resolved through the Foundation's dispute resolution process (Section 8).

### 6.3 Architectural and Foundational Work

The peer-review process shall give appropriate weight to architectural decisions, design work, and foundational contributions that may not be reflected in lines of code but that materially shape the Licensed Work.

---

## 7. Officers

### 7.1 Roles

The Foundation shall appoint officers as needed to conduct its operations. Officer roles, responsibilities, and appointment procedures shall be determined by ordinary resolution.

Until officer roles are formally established, the Founder shall fulfil all officer functions.

### 7.2 Appointment and Removal

Officers are appointed and removed by ordinary resolution. The Founder may appoint or remove any officer without a vote.

---

## 8. Dispute Resolution

### 8.1 Internal Mediation

Any dispute arising between members of the Foundation, or between a member and the Foundation itself, shall first be raised at a general meeting for discussion and resolution.

### 8.2 Formal Mediation

If the dispute is not resolved at a general meeting, any party may request formal mediation. The Foundation shall appoint a mediator acceptable to all parties, or failing agreement, an independent mediator selected by the Founder. Mediation shall be completed within 90 days of the request.

### 8.3 Legal Action

If mediation fails to resolve the dispute, any party may pursue legal remedies in accordance with the governing law specified in the OFA License (laws of England and Wales).

### 8.4 Costs

Each party bears their own costs in dispute resolution, unless the Foundation votes to cover costs from the operating fund.

---

## 9. Departure and Inactive Contributors

### 9.1 Voluntary Departure

A Contributor may resign from the Foundation at any time by written notice. Upon resignation, the Contributor's future revenue share entitlement ceases. Any accrued but unpaid revenue share for quarters in which the Contributor participated shall still be paid.

### 9.2 Inactivity

A Contributor who has not attended a quarterly revenue distribution meeting (in person, by video, or by written submission) for four consecutive quarters may be reclassified as Inactive by ordinary resolution.

Inactive Contributors retain their Contributor membership but are not entitled to revenue shares until they resume active participation.

### 9.3 Review

All decisions regarding departure and inactivity are subject to Foundation review and may be overridden by ordinary resolution. The Foundation shall consider the totality of a Contributor's history and circumstances before making determinations under this section.

---

## 10. Amendment

This Charter may be amended by a supermajority vote (75% of total voting shares) at a general meeting, provided that:

a) The proposed amendment has been circulated to all members at least 30 days in advance.

b) The Founder has not exercised a veto.

No amendment may remove or diminish the Founder's permanent 51% voting share or veto power.

---

## 11. Dissolution

### 11.1 Trigger

The Foundation may be dissolved by a supermajority vote (75% of total voting shares) with the Founder's explicit written consent.

### 11.2 Asset Distribution

Upon dissolution:

a) All outstanding financial obligations shall be settled.

b) Remaining funds shall be distributed to Contributors in proportion to their most recent annual revenue share allocation.

c) The intellectual property rights to the Licensed Work revert to the Founder.

### 11.3 Post-Dissolution Licensing

Following dissolution, the OFA License remains in effect. Existing commercial license agreements remain binding. The Founder assumes all rights and obligations of the Foundation under existing agreements.

---

## 12. Governing Law

This Charter shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of England and Wales.
